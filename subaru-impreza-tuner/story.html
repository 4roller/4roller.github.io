Function over everything

In all honesty, I didn't know what kind of car I was buying, only that it fit my initial buying criteria. Hatchback with good trunk space (I actually wanted a wagon), and good on gas. For the better part of a year I drove it stock.

Utility

Commuter, errands, family duties, the Subaru handles it all. There were gripes about 'weak engine' expressed here and there, but for the most part it was a workhorse. A great utility vehicle for any modern homesteader.

Enter Tuner

After dropping off the kids and the wife into a larger family suited vehicle, the shackles were removed. No longer does smooth, quiet and comfortable need to be adjectives that describe this car. Rice, I mean Race car would be the new charade.

Being a newb. The quest for performance has to start out being inexpensive, what is the best bang for the buck. The goal of the build is extract the soul of the car through mechanical modification, a frankenstein for betterment of passenger car performance.
