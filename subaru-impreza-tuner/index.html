What is it?

It's a Subaru!
More specifcally it's a USDM 2016 Subaru Impreza Sport Hatchback 2.0i CVT in Premium trim. These are the last Imprezas to be built in Gunma, Japan. I took delivery in Los Angeles, California.

Lineage

The GP7 hatchback chassis comes from a long line of Subaru wagons starting namely of the iconic first generation GF Outback Sport Wagon. The GG was the second generation 5 door wagon. Its direct predecessor, the 3rd generation Impreza GH(narrow) / GR (wide) hatchback shares a similar unibody design and has competed in rally and motorsports . You might say that the 4th generation GP(7) chassis also shares this motorsports pedigree. The same GP chassis is used in both the Impreza hatchback and the XV Crosstrek. The GT chassis code is the 5th generation 5-door wagon/hatchback. For all intents and purposes, the tuning and modificaitons contained in these pages are on the GP chassis but may also relate to other subaru models past and present.

Likewise VDC or Vehicle Dynamics Control is a traction control system. It works by monitoring wheel speed sensors for slippage reducing engine power and appling brakes to slipping wheels, to redirect power to wheels that grip. A very useful safety feature.

Why would I want to disable VDC?

Because why else would there be a button?

The driver of the Subaru can regain some engine and braking control. Note, With VDC off it is never fully disabled, but the system will no longer fully limit the engine output, and sensitivily at which the ABS kicks in will be muted. This is perfect if there is traction loss, say in slipperly snow or gravel, or pavement drift , when you need to engine power to push you through.
