Subaru Impreza Tuner - Engine - FB20B BOXER

Overview

The FB20B was designed as an economy engine, it has a peak horsepower and torque that are typical of similar engines of the same displacement. Being naturally aspirated and utilizing port injection it is meant to serve as a sort of workhorse, reliable and economical. It has horizontally apposed pistons which helps lower the center of gravity when placed in the engine bay. The name of this style of engine is the boxer.

Engine Specifications

  • Naturally Aspirated - port injection
  • 145 ft/lbs (197 N-m) @4200rpm
  • 148hp (110 kw) @6200rpm
  • 10.5:1 compression ratio
  • 2.0L Flat-4 cylinder
  • DOHC w/ DAVCS
  • 0w-20w oil

The FB20 is a little engine, its overall power is quite moderate. Being naturally aspirated, it would be difficult to add any significant numbers without a full-on rebuild and custom ECU tune.

Fret not, the small engine in stock form can still produce enough power to make for a fun drive. Typically most people feel comfortable using the lower rev ranges for everyday driving. However for spirited driving, the Naturally Aspirated engine enjoys high revs in order to produce peak power. 4.2k-6.2k is the elastic range of the engine.

Aside, the ECU (engine control unit) has a throttle map that is rather conservative from the factory. This is meant to increase fuel efficiency. However, the ECU has the ability to "learn" the drivers preference and can adjust up to ~25%. What this means is, if you want to drive spiritedly, then do so. The ECU will adjust timing (DAVCS) to increase the power. Over a number of miles, it will adjust accordingly. By and large, if you push the engine to its upper thresholds, it can produce more useable power and better performance.

Upgrades for Performance and Reliability

Typically naturally aspirated economy engines get little support in the aftermarket. There are options for building a more performant engine, but it is outside of the realm of what is feasible for an economy tuner.
